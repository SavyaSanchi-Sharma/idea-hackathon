## 4. Mathematical formulation

Throughout: $r \sim U[a,b]$ denotes uniform sampling; $\mathrm{ri}[a,b]$ uniform integer sampling; $\mathcal{N}(\mu,\sigma)$ Gaussian; $\mathrm{Bern}(p)$ Bernoulli.

### 4.1 Auth scheme resolution (extraction)

For an OpenAPI/Swagger operation with `security: [ {schemeName: []}, … ]`:

$$
\text{auth\_scheme} = \mathrm{join}_{|}\left(\, \mathrm{sort}\{\, \text{label}(s) : s \in \text{requirement} \}\right)
$$

where label is built from the scheme's `type`, `scheme`, and `flow` fields:

$$
\text{label}(s) = \begin{cases}
\text{type}:\text{scheme} & \text{if scheme is set (e.g. } \texttt{http:bearer}\text{)} \\
\text{type}:\text{flow} & \text{else if flow is set (e.g. } \texttt{oauth2:implicit}\text{)} \\
\text{type} & \text{otherwise (e.g. } \texttt{apiKey}\text{)}
\end{cases}
$$

Empty requirement `{}` yields `none`. Operations with no `security` and no spec-level `security` yield `none`.

### 4.2 Scenario sampling (lifecycle generator)

Each banking endpoint is assigned a scenario $S \in \{\text{active, deprecated, orphaned\_quiet, zombie, shadow}\}$ by categorical sampling:

$$
P(S = s) = w_s, \quad w = (0.50,\ 0.15,\ 0.10,\ 0.15,\ 0.10)
$$

Scenarios map to the 3-class lifecycle label + 2 boolean flags via a static table:

| scenario | lifecycle_state | is_zombie | is_shadow |
|---|---|---|---|
| active | active | 0 | 0 |
| deprecated | deprecated | 0 | 0 |
| orphaned_quiet | orphaned | 0 | 0 |
| **zombie** | orphaned | **1** | 0 |
| **shadow** | orphaned | 0 | **1** |

So the classifier (model 1) outputs the rubric-required 3 classes (active / deprecated / orphaned), while `is_zombie` and `is_shadow` are derived flags that the dashboard surfaces separately.

### 4.3 Feature distributions per scenario

Let $X_s$ denote the feature vector sampled for scenario $s$.

**active**
$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{in\_registry} &= 1, \quad \text{owner\_present} = 1 \\
\text{last\_seen\_days} &\sim \mathrm{ri}[0,2] \\
\text{call\_count\_7d} &\sim \mathrm{ri}[500,\ 50000] \\
\text{auth\_fail\_rate\_7d} &\sim U[0,\ 0.02] \\
\text{p95\_latency\_ms} &\sim \mathrm{ri}[40,\ 400] \\
\text{last\_deploy\_days} &\sim \mathrm{ri}[0,\ 60]
\end{aligned}
$$

**deprecated**
$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{in\_registry} &= 1, \quad \text{owner\_present} = 1 \\
\text{last\_seen\_days} &\sim \mathrm{ri}[0,14], \quad \text{call\_count\_7d} \sim \mathrm{ri}[10,\ 500] \\
\text{auth\_fail\_rate\_7d} &\sim U[0,\ 0.05], \quad \text{p95\_latency\_ms} \sim \mathrm{ri}[100,\ 800] \\
\text{last\_deploy\_days} &\sim \mathrm{ri}[180,\ 720]
\end{aligned}
$$

**orphaned_quiet**
$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{in\_registry} &= 1, \quad \text{owner\_present} = 0 \\
\text{last\_seen\_days} &\sim \mathrm{ri}[7,90], \quad \text{call\_count\_7d} \sim \mathrm{ri}[0,\ 100] \\
\text{auth\_fail\_rate\_7d} &\sim U[0.05,\ 0.30], \quad \text{p95\_latency\_ms} \sim \mathrm{ri}[200,\ 2000] \\
\text{last\_deploy\_days} &\sim \mathrm{ri}[365,\ 1500]
\end{aligned}
$$

**zombie** (orphaned + high traffic)
$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{in\_registry} &= 1, \quad \text{owner\_present} = 0 \\
\text{last\_seen\_days} &\sim \mathrm{ri}[0,2], \quad \text{call\_count\_7d} \sim \mathrm{ri}[3000,\ 30000] \\
\text{auth\_fail\_rate\_7d} &\sim U[0.02,\ 0.10], \quad \text{p95\_latency\_ms} \sim \mathrm{ri}[200,\ 1500] \\
\text{last\_deploy\_days} &\sim \mathrm{ri}[365,\ 1500]
\end{aligned}
$$

**shadow**
$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{in\_registry} &= 0, \quad \text{owner\_present} = 0 \\
\text{last\_seen\_days} &\sim \mathrm{ri}[0,7], \quad \text{call\_count\_7d} \sim \mathrm{ri}[50,\ 5000] \\
\text{auth\_fail\_rate\_7d} &\sim U[0.10,\ 0.50], \quad \text{p95\_latency\_ms} \sim \mathrm{ri}[50,\ 1500] \\
\text{last\_deploy\_days} &\sim \mathrm{ri}[0,\ 90]
\end{aligned}
$$

The ranges are designed so each feature contributes separability for at least one pair of classes (call_count separates orphaned_quiet from zombie; owner_present separates active+deprecated from orphaned+zombie+shadow; in_registry separates shadow from all others; last_deploy_days separates active from orphaned/zombie; etc.).

### 4.4 Endpoint mutation for shadow

For shadow rows, the banking path is rewritten:

$$
\text{endpoint} \leftarrow \mathrm{re.sub}(\texttt{/v}\backslash d+\texttt{/},\ \texttt{/internal/},\ \text{endpoint})
$$

So `/v1/accounts/{accountId}/balance` becomes `/internal/accounts/{accountId}/balance` — the recognizable signature of an undocumented internal deployment.

### 4.5 Auth coupling

- **shadow:** $\text{auth\_scheme} \leftarrow$ `"none"` with probability $0.6$, else preserved from source.
- **active:** if the source auth was `"none"` (which would be unrealistic for a healthy banking endpoint), it is upgraded by uniform sampling from `{apiKey, oauth2:implicit, http:bearer, http:basic|http:bearer}`. After this step no active row has `auth=none`.
- **deprecated/orphaned/zombie:** preserved from source.

### 4.6 Deprecated flag

$$
\text{deprecated\_flag} = \begin{cases}
1 & \text{if scenario} = \text{deprecated} \\
1 \text{ w.p. } 0.6,\ \text{source value otherwise} & \text{if scenario} = \text{zombie} \\
0 & \text{if scenario} = \text{shadow} \\
\text{source value} & \text{otherwise}
\end{cases}
$$

### 4.7 Risk score (model 2 label)

For each row:

$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{risk} =\ &30 \cdot (1 - \text{in\_registry}) \\
            +\ &40 \cdot \text{auth\_fail\_rate\_7d} \\
            +\ &20 \cdot \frac{\text{max\_cvss}}{10} \\
            +\ &15 \cdot (1 - \text{owner\_present}) \\
            +\ &10 \cdot \min\!\left(\frac{\text{last\_deploy\_days}}{1000},\ 1\right) \\
            +\ &15 \cdot \mathbb{1}[\text{auth\_scheme} = \text{none}] \\
            +\ &20 \cdot \mathbb{1}[\text{is\_zombie} = 1] \\
            +\ &\varepsilon, \quad \varepsilon \sim \mathcal{N}(0,\ 5)
\end{aligned}
$$

$$
\text{risk\_score} = \mathrm{clip}(\text{risk},\ 0,\ 100)
$$

Theoretical max (no clipping): $30 + 40\cdot 0.5 + 20 + 15 + 10 + 15 + 20 = 130$, which guarantees the clip ceiling is reachable. Empirically the distribution spans the full $[0, 100]$ range.

**Note:** because `risk_score` is a deterministic function of features (plus noise), a regressor trained on these features will fit near-perfectly. This is expected for synthetic data — the regressor proves the *pipeline* works, not generalization to real-world risk.

### 4.8 OWASP findings (rule-based)

Rule set $R$ — each predicate fires its category independently:

| OWASP category | predicate |
|---|---|
| **API1: BOLA** | `endpoint` matches `\{\w*[Ii]d\}` AND `auth_scheme` ∈ {`none`, `apiKey`, `basic`, `apiKey\|basic`} |
| **API2: Broken Authentication** | `auth_scheme = "none"` OR `auth_fail_rate_7d > 0.10` |
| **API4: Unrestricted Resource Consumption** | `p95_latency_ms > 1000` |
| **API8: Security Misconfiguration** | `max_cvss ≥ 7.0` |
| **API9: Improper Inventory Management** | `in_registry = 0`<br>OR `deprecated_flag = 1` AND `call_count_7d > 0`<br>OR `last_deploy_days > 365` AND `owner_present = 0` |
| **API10: Unsafe Consumption Of APIs** | `last_deploy_days > 720` AND `max_cvss ≥ 7.0` |

$\text{owasp\_findings} = \mathrm{join}_{|}(R)$, $\text{finding\_count} = |R|$.

### 4.9 Sequence generator (model 3 input)

For each endpoint with scenario $s$ (derived from the lifecycle row via `derive_scenario`), generate $T = 30$ daily samples.

**Base trajectories:** with $t \in [0, T)$ and $B$ the per-endpoint baseline:

| scenario | $B \sim$ | calls(t) | auth_fail(t) | p95(t) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| active | $\mathrm{ri}[800, 5000]$ | $B + \mathcal{N}(0, 0.05B)$ | $\mathcal{N}(0.01, 0.005)$ | $\mathcal{N}(120, 15)$ |
| deprecated | $\mathrm{ri}[200, 1500]$ | $B \cdot \mathrm{linspace}(1.0, 0.2, T) + \mathcal{N}(0, 0.05B)$ | $\mathcal{N}(0.03, 0.01)$ | $\mathcal{N}(250, 40)$ |
| orphaned_quiet | $\mathrm{ri}[0, 80]$ | $B + \mathcal{N}(0, 10)$ | $\mathcal{N}(0.15, 0.05)$ | $\mathcal{N}(600, 200)$ |
| zombie | $\mathrm{ri}[3000, 15000]$ | $B + \mathcal{N}(0, 0.08B)$ | $\mathcal{N}(0.05, 0.02)$ | $\mathcal{N}(450, 100)$ |
| shadow | $\mathrm{ri}[100, 2000]$ | $B + \mathcal{N}(0, 0.4B)$ (spiky) | $\mathcal{N}(0.25, 0.10)$ | $\mathcal{N}(400, 200)$ |

The `deprecated` trajectory uses $\mathrm{linspace}(1.0, 0.2, T)$, i.e. a linear decay from 100% to 20% of baseline over $T$ days — encoding the "graceful sunset" expectation.

**Anomaly injection.** With probability $p = \mathrm{ANOMALY\_RATE} = 0.10$, an endpoint receives a behavior shift:

$$
\text{shift} \sim \mathrm{ri}[T/3,\ 2T/3], \qquad m \sim \mathrm{Unif}\{0.1,\ 3.0\}
$$
$$
\text{calls}[t] \leftarrow m \cdot \text{calls}[t] \quad \forall\, t \geq \text{shift}
$$
$$
\text{anomaly} = \mathbb{1}[\text{shift applied}]
$$

So 10% of endpoints have either a $10\times$ drop or a $3\times$ spike sometime in the middle third of the window. The `anomaly` flag is constant across all 30 rows of the same endpoint.

Final clipping: $\text{calls} \in [0,\infty)$, $\text{auth\_fail} \in [0,1]$, $\text{p95} \in [10, \infty)$.

## 6. Caveats

1. **Label leakage in risk_score.** Model 2 will fit near-perfectly because `risk_score` is a deterministic function of the same features the model sees. This is a *pipeline correctness* demonstration, not a generalization claim. For an honest model 2 demo, either increase $\sigma$ on the noise term (currently 5), or hold out one feature (e.g., `max_cvss`) from training inputs.
2. **CVE coverage is 100%.** Every (runtime, version) in `RUNTIMES` has an entry in `CVE_DATA`, so every endpoint joins to a CVE and gets `max_cvss > 0`. In production this would be 30–60%. Drop a few entries from `CVE_DATA` if you want a more realistic distribution.
3. **`auth_scheme` carries from source spec.** Real banking APIs would have a tight, curated auth distribution (mostly oauth2/mTLS, very rarely apiKey). The current distribution reflects whatever the 50 source specs declared — fine as structural noise, but the dashboard should not present it as a literal bank auth survey.
4. **Anomaly rate is uniform across states.** In reality anomalies cluster in unstable populations (shadow/orphaned). Currently $\sim 10\%$ everywhere. To fix, condition the anomaly probability on scenario.
5. **Generator is deterministic on `SEED=42`.** Regenerating with a different seed will shift counts and individual feature values but preserve all distributions and sanity guarantees.
